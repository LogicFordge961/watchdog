Core proposition

It doesn’t replace your operating system. It doesn’t fight your workflow. It learns it.

Complete Awareness of Your Computer

WatchDog maintains a living map of your entire system.

It automatically discovers:

  • All installed applications
  • Portable programs
  • Games, including Steam titles
  • Software across all drives and storage devices
  • Applications installed in non-standard locations

Drive letters don’t matter. Folder layouts don’t matter. Even external or secondary drives are handled naturally. If an app exists on your system, WatchDog knows how to find it and how to launch it.

Nothing needs to be set up manually.

Launch Anything, Naturally

You don’t issue commands. You speak normally.

WatchDog understands intent when you say things like:

  • “Open Minecraft”
  • “Launch my Steam games”
  • “Close everything except Discord”
  • “Start what I usually use for gaming”
  • “Open the app I use for school”

It translates natural language into safe system actions without guessing recklessly. If something is ambiguous, WatchDog asks. If something can’t be done, it explains why. The system feels conversational, not scripted.

Deep Game Awareness, Including Steam

WatchDog treats games as first-class applications. It detects Steam installations across all drives and understands how each game launches. You can open games directly through WatchDog without opening Steam yourself, and it handles everything quietly in the background.

When games crash, WatchDog notices. When crashes repeat, WatchDog remembers. Over time, it builds stability profiles for individual games and recognizes when something isn’t normal.

Crash Detection That Learns

WatchDog doesn’t just log crashes. It understands them.

It distinguishes between normal app closures, freezes, hard crashes, and repeated failures. It observes what was running, what usually runs alongside it, and whether the crash follows a pattern. This allows WatchDog to provide meaningful insights instead of vague error messages. If a problem keeps happening, WatchDog doesn’t panic—it explains.

Behavioral Pattern Recognition

WatchDog quietly learns how you use your system. It notices habits such as apps you open together, programs you use at specific times of day, games you play regularly, and software you always close before starting something else.

From this, WatchDog begins offering helpful suggestions — optional, clear, and respectful — like:

  • “You usually play Minecraft around this time. Would you like me to launch it automatically?”
  • “This game tends to crash after long sessions. Want a warning before it happens again?”
  • “These apps are almost always used together.”

WatchDog never takes control without permission.

Smart Automation Without Intrusion

WatchDog automates only what you approve. Every suggestion explains what it will do, why it’s suggesting it, and how to disable it at any time. There are no hidden rules, no forced behaviors, and no surprises. Automation exists to reduce friction, not create it.

Real-Time System Monitoring

WatchDog keeps track of running applications, resource pressure, background activity, and system stability over time. This awareness allows it to make decisions based on context, not just commands. It understands what your system is doing right now, not just what you asked five seconds ago.

Private, Local, and Trustworthy

WatchDog runs entirely on your machine. There is no cloud dependency, no data uploads, no telemetry, no usage tracking, and no subscriptions. Your system data stays local. Your usage patterns stay private. WatchDog exists to help you, not to observe you for someone else.

Designed to Be Quietly Reliable

WatchDog doesn’t interrupt you. It doesn’t nag. It doesn’t pretend to be human. It waits for the moments where assistance actually matters—and then it helps clearly, calmly, and safely.

WatchDog is a system that understands your computer the way you do—only with perfect memory and endless patience.

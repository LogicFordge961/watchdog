What WatchDog does

A calm, tireless system sentinel — local, private, and predictable

WatchDog is a local, self-hosted system assistant focused on awareness and control. It runs on your machine, does not rely on cloud or paid APIs, and keeps your data local. Think of it as a system daemon with a brain rather than a cloud chatbot — it observes, indexes, and acts only when an explicit intent maps to a real capability.

Local and self-hosted

WatchDog runs entirely on your machine. Nothing is sent to the cloud by default — no external APIs, no remote telemetry. This design gives you predictable privacy and control.

System mapping & app discovery

Instead of assuming fixed install paths, WatchDog scans all mounted drives (primary, secondary, external) and builds a dynamic map of what actually exists. The app index reflects current reality: installs appear, uninstalls disappear, and new apps are discovered without manual configuration.

Natural-language control (intent-based)

Users express intent in plain language — e.g. “Open Discord”, “Launch my Steam game”, “Close Chrome”, “Why did Spotify crash?”. A lightweight local parser converts phrases to structured intents. If an intent maps to a supported capability it runs; otherwise WatchDog reports that it can't perform the action. There are no hallucinations and no guessing.

Process & crash tracking

•
Active monitoring

WatchDog tracks running processes and detects unexpected exits, distinguishing user closes from crashes.

•
Crash history

Keeps short-term histories (e.g., “Discord crashed three times today”) and can optionally restart or warn on repeated failures.

•
Context-aware

Records what happened just before a crash to aid diagnosis and triage.

Steam & game support

WatchDog treats Steam as a launcher ecosystem: it can detect Steam libraries across drives, read installed game manifests, and launch games directly by name without first opening Steam. Games are handled as executables with metadata, enabling future features like per-game crash tracking and playtime awareness.

Architecture & principles

Modular design

Separate files for discovery, launching, parsing, monitoring, Steam integration, and capability reporting let you replace or extend pieces without rewriting the whole system.

Capability awareness

At runtime WatchDog knows what it can and cannot do (missing permissions, missing apps, absent Steam) and reports that honestly instead of failing silently.

Non-destructive by default

WatchDog performs safe operations (open, close, observe). Dangerous actions are excluded unless explicitly added later — prioritizing daily safety and experimentation.

Staged roadmap

WatchDog is intentionally staged: early stages build stability and trust, middle stages add intelligence and prediction, and later stages explore autonomy (proactive warnings, optimization suggestions, multi-device awareness). Even at Stage 2 it already functions as a universal app launcher, Steam launcher, system observer, and crash monitor.

In short

WatchDog is not trying to be human — it is trying to be reliable. It observes your system, indexes reality, translates plain-language intent into safe actions, and provides honest capability reporting so you can rely on it.
